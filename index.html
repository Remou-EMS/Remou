The challenge

The energy transition’s hidden problem.

Solar, BESS, EV charging and flexible loads often operate in isolation, leading to curtailment, poor timing, and missed flexibility opportunities.

Disconnected control

Assets are run independently without unified setpoints or visibility.

Static schedules

Charging and discharging can miss optimal windows.

Vendor lock-in

Closed EMS platforms limit integration and innovation.

Limited market access

Flexibility services are difficult to access with rigid systems.

Across recent studies, structured energy management in C&I sites is associated with energy performance improvements ranging from low single-digit annual gains to 20%+ in advanced cases, depending on implementation maturity, data availability, and the use of controllable assets—underscoring the value typically left unrealized in unmanaged operations.

Study A ISO 50001 certification and energy intensity reduction in manufacturing firms, Energy Economics, Elsevier, 2024.
Study B Kanneganti, R. et al., Energy performance improvement through ISO 50001–based energy management systems, Energy Policy, Elsevier, 2023.
